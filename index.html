Understanding Your Kidneys

Vital organs that perform essential functions to keep you healthy

What Are Kidneys?

The kidneys are two bean-shaped organs, each about the size of a fist. They are located just below the rib cage, one on each side of your spine. Every day, your kidneys filter about 120 to 150 quarts of blood to produce about 1 to 2 quarts of urine, composed of wastes and extra fluid.

Key Functions of Kidneys

Waste Filtration

Remove waste products and excess fluids from your body through urine

Electrolyte Balance

Maintain a stable balance of body electrolytes like sodium, potassium, and phosphate

Blood Pressure Regulation

Produce hormones that regulate blood pressure and red blood cell production

Bone Health

Activate vitamin D to maintain strong bones and healthy chemical balance

Common Kidney Diseases

Understanding kidney conditions and their treatments

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)

CKD is a progressive loss of kidney function over months or years. Early detection can help prevent progression to kidney failure.

Symptoms:

  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Swelling in hands and feet
  • Shortness of breath
  • Persistent itching

Treatment Options

Our comprehensive approach to CKD management includes medication, lifestyle changes, and advanced renal replacement therapies.

Our Services:

  • Medication management
  • Dietary planning
  • Dialysis services
  • Transplantation evaluation

Kidney Stones

Hard deposits of minerals and salts that form inside your kidneys. They can be extremely painful when passing through the urinary tract.

Symptoms:

  • Severe pain in side and back
  • Pain that radiates to lower abdomen
  • Painful urination
  • Pink, red or brown urine

Treatment & Prevention

We offer advanced diagnostic and treatment options for kidney stones, including minimally invasive procedures.

Our Approach:

  • Shock wave lithotripsy
  • Ureteroscopy
  • Percutaneous nephrolithotomy
  • Dietary counseling for prevention

Kidney Infections (Pyelonephritis)

A type of urinary tract infection that generally begins in your urethra or bladder and travels to one or both kidneys. Prompt medical attention is needed to prevent permanent damage.

Symptoms:

  • Fever and chills
  • Back, side or groin pain
  • Abdominal pain
  • Frequent, painful urination
  • Cloudy, dark, or bloody urine
  • Nausea and vomiting

Treatment & Prevention

We provide comprehensive care for kidney infections, from accurate diagnosis to effective treatment plans tailored to each patient's needs.

Our Approach:

  • Antibiotic therapy tailored to infection type
  • Hospitalization for severe cases with IV antibiotics
  • Pain management strategies
  • Follow-up care to prevent recurrence
  • Education on urinary tract health

Kidney Cancer

Kidney cancer begins when healthy cells in one or both kidneys change and grow out of control, forming a mass called a tumor. Early detection significantly improves treatment outcomes.

Symptoms:

  • Blood in urine (hematuria)
  • Persistent pain in side or lower back
  • Lump or mass in side or abdomen
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue and loss of appetite
  • Fever that comes and goes

Treatment Options

Our multidisciplinary team provides personalized treatment plans for kidney cancer using the latest evidence-based approaches and technologies.

Our Services:

  • Surgical options (partial or radical nephrectomy)
  • Minimally invasive procedures (cryoablation, radiofrequency ablation)
  • Targeted therapy and immunotherapy
  • Radiation therapy for advanced cases
  • Clinical trials for innovative treatments
  • Comprehensive survivorship care

Prevention & Healthy Habits

Protecting your kidney health through lifestyle choices

Stay Hydrated

Drinking plenty of water helps your kidneys clear sodium, urea, and toxins from the body

Eat Healthy

A balanced diet low in salt and processed foods reduces kidney strain and prevents damage

Exercise Regularly

Physical activity helps maintain healthy blood pressure and reduces diabetes risk

Monitor Medications

Avoid excessive use of NSAIDs and always follow dosage instructions

Avoid Smoking

Smoking reduces blood flow to the kidneys and increases cancer risk

Regular Check-ups

Annual screenings are crucial if you have diabetes, hypertension, or family history
